Bring your homemade or homegrown items for a barter style swap! Meet fellow foodies, bakers, gardeners, farmers and local like-minded people. Bring an old friend or come and make new friends. Serving the Wilmington, Delaware area.

Who are we?

Wilmington Food Swap is a community of local foodies who share a passion for homemade, homegrown, or foraged foods.

Starting Spring 2019, we will meet quarterly to swap and sample each others kitchen creations. While this event is open to anyone, registration in advance is required to attend.

For information about how you can help, see our Support Us page.

What is a food swap?

A food swap is a quarterly event where you and fellow foodies meet to exchange and share their homemade, homegrown, or foraged foods with each other. Food swaps are a great way to change up the homemade and homegrown foods in your pantry, while getting to meet local foodies like yourself in the process.

Take a minute and review our Tips For Successful Swapping

What kinds of food should I bring?

In truth, the sky is the limit. A loaf of bread, eggs if you raise chickens or vegetables from your garden. Jams, jellies and marmalade are frequently swapped at these events. Get creative with pasta, granola, kombucha, or condiments. Candies and cookies are great as well, but be mindful of portion size. Someone who made a pint of jam probably would not want to make a swap for just one or two cookies. Whatever you decide to bring, make sure that it’s packaged in bags or jars and properly labeled.

It's recommended you bring five to ten items you wish to swap, as well as samples or a potluck dish to share with the group.
